Multi-Agent Boid Optimization

I have had an interest in the "Boid" model for a a while, and my recent course in artificial intelligence gave me a chance to research and write about it. My paper touched on the behaviour of these large chaotic swarms, and the optimization of the calculations involved. I utilized Unity 3D, C#, and HLSL to perform the simulations with my final and optimal solution a mixture of CPU and GPU calculations working together. I learned a lot over the course of this paper, and I look forward to diving deeper into AI this Winter 2021 semester.

Mobile Game

Mobile applications have always interested me. The ability to build applications and have them available in my pocket is very intriguing, and I feel like it opens so many doors. My current project utilizes coherent procedural noise algorithms like Simplex, Perlin, and Voronoi noise to generate infinite landscapes for the player to fly over in their hot air balloon as they complete delivery and mapping tasks. It is a casual mobile game that features infinite noise generation, a wind simulation system, a damage simulation system, and a quest system all wrapped up with minimalistic graphics. I plan to release it to the public early 2021.
